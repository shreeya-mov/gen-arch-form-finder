How It Works

Genetic Algorithm Overview: This project uses a genetic algorithm to evolve architectural silhouettes toward optimal designs based on multiple fitness criteria.

Fitness Function: Each shape is scored based on three metrics:

Stability (40%)

Measures how compact and balanced the form is. More stable shapes have control points closer to their center.

Symmetry (30%)

Evaluates the left-right symmetry of the silhouette, favoring more symmetric architectural forms.

Curvature Smoothness (30%)

Rewards smooth, continuous curves over sharp, jagged transitions in the form.

Evolution Process: In each generation, the top 50% of shapes are selected as parents. New offspring are created by crossing over parent forms and applying random mutations. Over 40 generations, this process converges toward highly optimized architectural silhouettes.
